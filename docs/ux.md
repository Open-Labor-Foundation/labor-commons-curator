# Experience — Labor Commons Specialist Contract

This contract has no UI of its own — it's consumed by other platforms — so
"experience" here means how the contract's *behavior* is felt by each person
who touches it, through whatever interface a consuming platform provides.

## The publisher's experience

Authoring or generating a new record should feel like making a claim you'll
have to defend, not filling out a form. The certification gate means a
submission isn't "done" the moment it's written — it's done when its own
stated claims have been tested and held up. A rejected certification should
come back with *which* claim failed and *why*, not a generic failure.

## The team-assembler's experience

Browsing to build a roster should surface coverage honestly: which areas are
well-matched, and which are gaps. A bulk match that quietly drops uncovered
areas (rather than naming them) would undermine the trust this whole contract
is built to earn. The experience should feel like getting an honest map of
coverage, not a forced-complete roster.

## The on-demand requester's experience

A single request should either come back with a specialist that actually
fits, or come back with an honest "nothing fits well" — never a
forced/best-guess match presented as confident. Getting told "no" is a better
experience than getting a bad answer presented as a good one.

## The end user's experience (once a specialist is activated)

Deliberately platform-defined, not part of this contract — whether the
interaction feels like a single clean handoff of a result or an ongoing
conversation depends entirely on the activating platform. What *is* part of
this contract, and should be felt consistently regardless of platform: if a
task drifts outside the specialist's lane mid-use, the handoff to a better-fit
neighbor should feel deliberate and explained, not like a silent failure or a
confidently wrong answer.

## Mood / tone

Throughout: **honesty over confidence**. Every point above — rejected
certification, gap reporting, no-match, handoff — trades a moment of "no" or
"not quite" for avoiding a bigger failure later (an unqualified answer trusted
as qualified). That tradeoff is the emotional core of the contract.

## Accessibility

Not discussed during idea capture — no accessibility requirements were raised
for how a downstream platform should render matches, handoffs, or results.
Flagged here rather than assumed, since accessibility of activation platforms
is out of this contract's scope but shouldn't be silently dropped when those
platforms are actually built.
